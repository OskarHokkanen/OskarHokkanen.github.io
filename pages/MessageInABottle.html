Message in a Bottle

A mindful messaging experience connecting strangers through the cosmic ocean

About the Project

Message in a Bottle reimagines digital communication by slowing it down and making it intentional. This prototype explores how we might connect with strangers in a more meaningful, meditative way, free from the constant notifications and instant replies that dominate modern messaging. Message in a Bottle strives to illustrate uncertainty as a positive quality.

The experience is designed around patience and ritual. Users discover messages floating in a cosmic ocean, hold their hand on the screen to "retrieve" them, and craft thoughtful replies knowing that responses may arrive days, weeks, or never at all.

This project questions the instant, certain feedback loops of communication and instead pictures a slower, more thoughtful way of communicating with each other.

Based on Rider Spoke by Blast Theory

As part of a challenge we were asked to redesign one of Blast Theory's projects, finding a change in that project and keeping or extendig the change to be exclusively online.

Rider spoke is a art project where people traveled around city streets on bikes, traveling to different locations and recording messages. Other riders travel to the same locations and find their messages and listen to them. From this experience we found many changes, however, one stood out.

Highlight connection and communication between anonymous people.

Uncertainty, Slow technology & Mystique

While keeping the change i also wanted to experiment with concepts of exploring uncertainty. The Rider Spoke project is a very different way of communicating with people than what is today where we instantly communicate with each other in seconds. Message in a bottle strives to take a step back from todays instant messaging slowing it down, giving time for reflection and uncertainty.

Design decisions has been drawn from my understanding of uncertainty, trying to illustrate that there are no commitments and no expectations on recieving or answering a message. The user can decide if they want to answer a message or just let it drift by. Instead of seeing uncertainty as risky the design communicates that it is fine and that it might be needed in order to slow down and reflect.

The experience was also inspired by Slow Technology, to design technology for reflection. This was for example done by having interactions such as opening a bottle take time, and forcing the person opening it to hold their finger down until it is open.

Visual Overview

Welcome & Discovery

The journey begins with an invitation to slow down and connect. Users enter a mystical cosmic ocean where messages appear as glowing bottles drifting through space, waiting to be discovered.

  • Mystical aesthetic sets the initial tone
  • Bottles float , encouraging exploration
  • Messages can only be found if they are close to your phones physical locations, there is an indication at the top when bottles are nearby

Read & Respond

Users hold their hand on the screen to retrieve messages, a deliberate ritual that makes the user wait in the moment.

  • Hold gesture forces the user to slow down and wait
  • Messages can drift away if they are too far away from you
  • Inspiration from calm and zen-like wording is used in the experience, for example the text inspiring the person to reply to a message

Write & Release

The journey of your messages begins with writing your own thoughts, and then sending it of into the world.

  • Users write their own reflections or questions
  • Send them away with no intention of getting a response
